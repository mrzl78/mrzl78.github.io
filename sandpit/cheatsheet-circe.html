CheatSheet Circe

Facts

Madeline Miller, Circe, 2018
Genre: #classics #feminist

Extracts

Extract 1 (1/3-middle part), on Crete. Circe has helped Pasiphaë give birth to the Minotaur
Relationships of the sisters? (not very good... Pasiphaë is manipulative and hungry for power, careless about any kind of relationships)

Themes

  • god vs. mortal
  • outcast because different
  • magic
  • being a woman / change of perspective

Characters

Questions

  1. How does Circe reflect on the differences between mortals and gods? In what ways are men more threatening than the gods? With whom does she feel more at ease?
  2. Which of the women in Circe’s life have the most impact on her life and choices? Who does she identify with the most and why?
  3. How does Circe’s witchcraft give her strength? How does she feel about the traditional expectation of feminine fragility?
  4. How does Circe change from the beginning of the book to the end? Why do you think she chooses her ultimate path?
  5. p. 325: “We are not our blood.” A repetition from an earlier exchange with Telemachus. How does the novel reflect this thought?
